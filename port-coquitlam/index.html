Broken Garage Door Spring Replacement in Port Coquitlam

A broken torsion spring is the most common reason a garage door suddenly won't open. If you heard a loud bang in the garage and now the door feels extremely heavy or won't lift at all, you almost certainly have a snapped spring. Don't try to force the door with the opener — you'll burn out the motor or strip the gears. Call us for same-day spring replacement across Port Coquitlam.

We replace torsion springs in matched pairs using high-cycle 25,000+ cycle oil-tempered springs that come with a lifetime warranty. Every spring replacement includes proper turns set to your door's exact weight, a fresh centre bearing if needed, full balance test, and lubrication. Most PoCo spring jobs are completed in 60–90 minutes on the same day you call.

Garage Door Cable Replacement in Port Coquitlam

Garage door lifting cables are a critical safety part that wear out from rust, fraying, and age — especially in Port Coquitlam's wet climate. If one cable has come off the drum, you can see rust or broken strands, or the door is hanging crooked, the cables need to be replaced immediately. A snapped cable can cause the door to drop violently and damage panels, vehicles, or worse.

We replace garage door cables in pairs with new galvanized aircraft-grade lifting cables sized to your exact door height. Every cable replacement includes re-spooling both drums, inspecting and re-tensioning the springs, rebalancing the door, and a full safety check. Most PoCo cable replacements take 45–60 minutes on-site — an affordable repair that prevents far more expensive damage.

Off-Track Garage Door Repair in Port Coquitlam

A garage door that has come off its track is an emergency — the door can be unstable, panels can bend, and the opener can be seriously damaged if you keep cycling it. The most common causes in PoCo are vehicle impact, a snapped cable, broken rollers, or a loose track bracket. Whatever the cause, stop operating the door and call us right away.

Our off-track repair service includes safely re-seating the door, replacing damaged rollers, hinges, brackets, or cables, straightening or replacing bent track sections, realigning to spec, and a full balance and safety test. Most off-track jobs in Port Coquitlam are resolved in a single same-day visit. Emergency service available evenings and weekends.

Garage Door Bottom Seal & Weather Stripping Replacement

If you see daylight under your closed garage door, feel a cold draft, or find water and leaves blowing into the garage after PoCo rain, your bottom seal needs to be replaced. Garage door bottom seals (also called bottom rubber, astragal, or weather seal) crack from UV, freeze, and tear over time — and once they fail, your garage loses its seal against weather, pests, and dirt.

We replace bottom seals, side seals, and top weather stripping on every type of garage door. We stock T-style, bulb-style, and brush seals in every common size, plus full aluminum retainers if yours is corroded. Most bottom seal replacements in Port Coquitlam take 30–45 minutes and dramatically improve garage warmth, cleanliness, and energy efficiency.

LiftMaster & Chamberlain Gear & Sprocket Repair

If your LiftMaster, Chamberlain, or Craftsman opener hums when you press the button but the door doesn't move — or you hear loud grinding from the motor head — the drive gear is stripped. This is the single most common opener repair we do in Port Coquitlam. Don't replace a perfectly good opener over a $30 part; in most cases a gear-and-sprocket kit will give you another 10+ years of service.

We carry full gear-and-sprocket kits for every LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Sears, and Craftsman model on the truck. Our repair includes pulling the motor head, cleaning out the old gear shavings, installing the new helical gear and sprocket, re-greasing, and a full force/limit reset. About a 45-minute on-site repair anywhere in PoCo.

Garage Door Opener Chain Repair & Replacement

If your garage door opener chain has come off the rail, fallen slack, or the motor runs without moving the trolley, we repair it the same day in Port Coquitlam. Chains derail because the chain has stretched over years of use, the trolley has snapped, or the drive sprocket teeth have worn down. We service every chain-drive opener — LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Craftsman, Genie, and older 1/2 HP, 3/4 HP, and 1 HP units.

Our chain-drive repair service includes replacing or re-tensioning the chain, swapping out the trolley if broken, replacing worn rail and sprockets, lubrication, and a full safety re-set. If your opener is 15+ years old we'll give you an honest cost comparison between repair and a new myQ smart opener — we never push a replacement when a repair makes sense.
