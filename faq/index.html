The most common cause is a broken spring or cable. When a spring breaks, the opener motor struggles to lift the full door weight and stalls. This is a safety issue — don't force the door or try to operate it. Call us and we'll diagnose and repair it the same day.

This is usually a close limit switch issue or misaligned safety sensors. The opener's limit switch tells it where the floor is — if it's set too high, the door reverses before reaching the ground. Misaligned photo-sensors near the floor can also trigger an auto-reversal. Both are easy to fix.

Most quality residential openers last 10–15 years with proper maintenance. Signs it's time for replacement include: loud grinding noises, slow response time, frequent remote failures, or if the unit lacks modern safety features. We can advise whether repair or replacement makes more financial sense.

For residential doors, an annual service visit is recommended. This includes lubricating moving parts, checking spring tension, inspecting cables and rollers, and testing safety features. A $100–$150 maintenance visit can prevent a $500+ emergency repair.

We strongly advise against DIY spring repair. Garage door springs are under extreme tension — a torsion spring stores enough energy to cause serious injury or death if it releases uncontrolled. This is one job where professional service is genuinely important for your safety.
