Warehouse Garage Door Service — Common Questions

What does a warehouse garage door maintenance contract cost?

Tri-Cities warehouse maintenance contracts typically run $80–$140 per door per visit, depending on door type, height and access. A standard 18-door facility on our Bi-Annual plan averages $2,400 per year all-in — usually paid back inside the first emergency call avoided. We always quote per-facility after a free site walk.

How fast do you respond to an emergency dock door failure?

Standard commercial response is same-day during business hours. Bi-Annual contract clients get a guaranteed 2-hour on-site SLA. Quarterly Enterprise contract clients get a 1-hour SLA, 24/7, including weekends and statutory holidays.

Do you service warehouse doors after hours?

Yes. We run a 24/7 emergency dispatch line for contract clients. After-hours service for non-contract clients is available at a published premium rate — we will quote it before dispatching.

Can you work on commercial doors we did not install?

Yes — the majority of our warehouse contracts are on doors installed by other vendors. There is no penalty for switching, and we will inventory every door on the first PM visit so you have a clean asset register going forward.

What brands of commercial doors do you service?

We service every major commercial overhead door and operator brand in BC: Richards-Wilcox, Steel-Craft, Garaga Commercial, Hormann, Wayne Dalton Commercial, Northwest Door, Clopay Commercial, Rytec, Albany high-speed, and LiftMaster, FAAC and Manaras commercial operators.

Do you provide WorkSafeBC documentation?

Yes. Every PM visit produces a written condition report per door. Tier 3 Enterprise contracts include an annual WorkSafeBC compliance audit covering safety photo eyes, safety edges, emergency release cords, panel integrity and pinch-point guards.

How long does a typical warehouse maintenance visit take?

Approximately 20–35 minutes per door for the full 21-point inspection. A 20-door facility is typically completed in a single day by a 2-tech crew, scheduled around your shift change to avoid disruption.

How much downtime should I expect during scheduled service?

Each door is offline for 15–25 minutes during its inspection. We work bay-by-bay so you never lose more than one door at a time, and we schedule around your shipping windows. Outside of those minutes, the rest of the dock keeps running.
