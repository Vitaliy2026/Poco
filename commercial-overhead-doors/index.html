What We Service

Three Commercial Failures We Fix Every Week

These are the calls that keep our trucks moving across Coquitlam, Port Coquitlam and Port Moody. If your door is doing one of these, we have the part on the truck today.

Dead Commercial Opener

Burnt motors, blown capacitors, stripped gears and seized chain drives on LiftMaster, Manaras-Opera, Micanan and Chamberlain Commercial operators. Same-day replacement when an opener is unsalvageable.

Broken Torsion Spring

Commercial doors cycle far more than residential. When a spring snaps, the door becomes immovable. We carry 2", 2-1/4" and 2-5/8" ID high-cycle springs — 25K, 50K and 100K cycle ratings on the truck.

Roll-Up Door Service

Wayne Dalton, Kinnear, CornellCookson and Cookson roll-up doors and grilles. Barrel re-springing, slat replacement, bottom bar, locking and operator service for parkade and bay roll-ups.

Diagnose Before Quoting

Commercial Opener Diagnostics & Board Repair

Most commercial opener problems are not the motor. They are the control board, limit switches, safety-edge wiring, or the auxiliary relay. We open the operator, test inputs and outputs at the board, and isolate the failure before quoting parts.

That keeps the bill honest and the downtime short. Logic-board programming, limit-cam replacement, radio receiver swap, safety loop repair and access-control re-integration after a board change — all done on site.

Common Questions From Tri-Cities Facility Managers

How fast can you respond to a commercial overhead door emergency?

Within one hour during business hours anywhere in Coquitlam, Port Coquitlam or Port Moody, and same-day for surrounding Tri-Cities areas. After hours, we run a 24/7 on-call line for stuck doors, lockouts and security situations — call 778-831-0239 and a technician will return your call within 15 minutes.

My bay door won't open. Should I keep pressing the button?

No. If the door is not moving, something mechanical is preventing it — usually a broken spring, snapped cable, or jammed track. Continuing to press the opener will burn out the motor or strip the gears, turning a $400 spring job into a $1,800 opener replacement. Pull the manual release cord (red), secure the door so it cannot fall, and call us.

Do you carry commercial parts on the truck?

Yes. Our service trucks stock high-cycle commercial torsion springs (2", 2-1/4", 2-5/8" ID), 7x19 aircraft cables, commercial drums, end bearings, rollers, hinges, weatherstripping and the LiftMaster, Manaras and Micanan opener parts that fit the majority of commercial doors in the Tri-Cities. Most jobs are first-visit fixes.

Can you service a door that another company installed?

Yes — we service every brand of commercial overhead door regardless of who installed it: Wayne Dalton, CHI, Amarr, Clopay, Richards-Wilcox, Garaga, Kinnear, CornellCookson, Steel-Craft and others. We are also familiar with all major commercial opener brands.

Do you offer service contracts for facilities with multiple doors?
